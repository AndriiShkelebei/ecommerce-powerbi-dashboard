# E-commerce Customer Retention & Delivery Impact

## Overview

This project analyzes customer retention, revenue trends, delivery performance, and customer satisfaction using the Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist.

The main goal of the project was to understand why customer retention is very low and whether delivery performance has an impact on customer satisfaction and repeat purchases.

The analysis was performed using SQL for data preparation and business metrics calculation, and Power BI for dashboard visualization.

---

## Business Problem

E-commerce companies rely not only on acquiring new customers, but also on retaining existing ones.

During the analysis, I identified that customer retention was very low: only around 3% of customers made more than one purchase. This means that the business depends heavily on new customer acquisition rather than repeat purchases.

The project focuses on answering the following question:

**Why is customer retention so low, and does delivery performance affect customer satisfaction and repeat customer behavior?**

---

## Business Questions

This project answers the following business questions:

- What is the customer retention rate?
- How many customers make repeat purchases?
- How much revenue is generated by returning customers?
- What percentage of orders are delivered late?
- How does delivery performance affect customer reviews?
- Which product categories generate the most revenue?
- Which product categories have better repeat purchase potential?
- Is low retention caused by delivery problems, tracking issues, or actual customer behavior?

---

## Data

Dataset: Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist  
Source: Kaggle  
Period analyzed: January 2017 – August 2018  
Dataset size: ~100,000 orders with customer, payment, product, delivery, and review data

### Data Adjustments

To make the analysis more reliable, I applied the following adjustments:

- Removed early low-volume months
- Excluded incomplete late months
- Aggregated order-level revenue
- Aggregated payment data by order
- Aggregated review scores by order
- Created delivery status based on actual and estimated delivery dates
- Segmented customers into new and returning customers

## Methodology

The project was structured as a business analysis case.

First, I prepared the analytical dataset in SQL using joins, CTEs, and aggregations. I combined order, customer, payment, product, delivery, and review data into analysis-ready tables.

Then, I calculated key business metrics, including revenue, monthly revenue growth, average order value, returning customer rate, delivery delay rate, and average review score.

After that, I performed deeper customer retention analysis to check whether low retention was related to delivery delays, product categories, customer tracking issues, or actual customer behavior.

Finally, I visualized the results in an interactive Power BI dashboard.

## Key Insights

### 1. Customer retention is very low

Only around 3% of customers made more than one purchase.

This means that approximately 97% of customers purchased only once. The business relies mostly on new customers rather than repeat buyers.

### 2. Returning customers generate a small share of revenue

Returning customers contribute only around 5.8% of total revenue.

This shows that repeat purchases have a limited impact on total business performance.

### 3. Delivery delays strongly reduce customer satisfaction

Delivery performance has a clear impact on customer reviews.

Average review score:

- On-time delivery: ~4.2
- Delayed delivery: ~2.6

This means that delayed orders are strongly associated with lower customer satisfaction.

### 4. Delivery delays are not the main reason for low retention

Although delayed delivery negatively affects customer satisfaction, it does not fully explain low customer retention.

The retention problem appears to be broader and connected to customer behavior, product category structure, and a business model based mostly on one-time purchases.

### 5. Revenue grew rapidly in 2017 and stabilized in 2018

The business showed strong revenue growth during 2017.

However, in 2018, revenue stabilized at around $1M per month, which may indicate that growth becomes harder when the company depends mainly on acquiring new customers.

### 6. High-revenue categories do not always have high retention

Some product categories generate significant revenue, but still have low repeat purchase rates.

This suggests that high sales volume does not automatically lead to strong customer retention.

---

## Main Conclusion

Low customer retention is not caused only by delivery issues or data tracking problems.

The analysis suggests that low retention reflects real customer behavior and a business model heavily dependent on one-time purchases and continuous acquisition of new customers.

Delivery delays reduce customer satisfaction, but the main retention challenge is broader: the company needs stronger post-purchase engagement, better repeat purchase incentives, and more focus on categories with higher retention potential.

---

## Business Impact

The analysis shows several important business risks:

- Heavy dependence on new customers increases marketing costs
- Low retention limits long-term revenue growth
- Revenue growth becomes difficult to scale
- Delivery delays reduce customer satisfaction
- One-time purchases dominate customer behavior
- Returning customers generate only a small share of revenue

---

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, I would recommend the following actions:

- Track customer retention as a core business KPI
- Improve post-purchase communication with customers
- Use email campaigns, promotions, and loyalty programs to encourage repeat purchases
- Focus on product categories with higher repeat purchase potential
- Increase cross-category recommendations to encourage additional purchases
- Continue improving delivery performance to protect customer satisfaction
- Analyze customer cohorts to better understand repeat purchase behavior over time
